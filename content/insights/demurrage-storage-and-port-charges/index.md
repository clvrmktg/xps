Delays in the import process are often seen as an inconvenience.

In practice, they come with a cost.

By the time a shipment arrives in The Bahamas, it is already on a timeline. If it is not cleared and collected within that window, additional charges begin to apply.

These costs are commonly referred to as **demurrage and storage**.

## What are demurrage and storage charges?

Demurrage and storage are charges applied when cargo remains at the port, terminal, or airline facility beyond the allowed free period.

While the terms are sometimes used interchangeably, they generally refer to:

- **Demurrage** — charges applied by the shipping line for containers that remain at the port beyond the free time  
- **Storage** — charges applied by the port, terminal, or airline for cargo that has not been collected  

Both are time-based and increase the longer the cargo remains uncleared or uncollected.

## Demurrage and Detention

Demurrage and storage apply while cargo remains at the port or terminal.

Once a container is collected and leaves the port, those charges stop. However, a different charge may begin if the container is not returned within the allowed time.

This is commonly referred to as **detention**.

Detention charges are applied by the shipping line for the use of the container outside the port. They continue to accumulate until the empty container is returned.

Like demurrage, these charges increase over time and can become significant if delays are not managed.

## When charges begin

Most shipments are allowed a limited number of **free days** after arrival.

Once this period expires, charges begin to accumulate daily.

The exact free time and rates vary depending on:

- The carrier or shipping line  
- The type of cargo (containerized, LCL, or air)  
- The facility where the cargo is stored  

Importers are not always notified when free time expires, so delays in processing can quickly become costly.

## Why delays happen

In many cases, these charges are not caused by the port or carrier, but by delays in clearing the shipment.

Common causes include:

- Missing Bills of Lading or Air Waybills  
- Missing or incorrect invoices  
- Use of pro forma invoices instead of final commercial invoices  
- Incomplete or unclear product descriptions  
- Missing freight information  
- Consignee details that do not match across documents  
- Delays in Click2Clear registration  
- Outstanding freight or local charges preventing carrier release  

Even small issues can delay the preparation or approval of an entry, allowing charges to accumulate.

## The connection to customs clearance

Demurrage and storage charges are often a downstream effect of issues earlier in the process.

If documents are incomplete or inconsistent, the broker cannot prepare the entry promptly. If the entry is delayed or queried, Customs cannot release the cargo.

As outlined in [From Ship to Release: How Imports Actually Clear Customs in The Bahamas](/customs-clearance-bahamas/), each step in the process depends on the one before it.

Delays at any stage can result in additional costs.

## Carrier release and customs release

Even after an entry is approved, cargo cannot be collected until both releases are in place.

The carrier must release the shipment after freight and local charges are paid, and Customs must release the entry after duties and VAT are settled.

If either of these is delayed, cargo remains at the terminal and charges may continue to accrue.

## How costs escalate

Demurrage and storage charges are typically calculated on a daily basis.

What begins as a short delay can quickly become significant, particularly for:

- Full container loads (FCL)  
- High-volume LCL shipments  
- Time-sensitive or high-value goods  

In some cases, the cost of delays can exceed the cost of the original freight.

## Reducing the risk of charges

Most demurrage and storage costs can be avoided with proper preparation.

This includes:

- Ensuring all documents are complete and consistent before shipment  
- Using final commercial invoices instead of pro formas  
- Confirming consignee details match across all documents  
- Registering in Click2Clear in advance  
- Providing required permits before arrival  
- Settling freight and local charges promptly  

Clear communication between the importer, supplier, and broker is essential.

## In simple terms

Delays in clearing or collecting cargo result in additional charges.

These costs are not random—they are the result of time lost during the process.

When shipments are properly prepared and processed without delay, these charges are usually avoided.
