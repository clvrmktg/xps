After a shipment is cleared by Customs, the next step is getting the goods to their final destination.

In many cases in The Bahamas, this movement happens quickly.

Cargo is typically collected from the Nassau Container Port (NCP) at Arawak Cay or the Gladstone Freight Terminal (GFT) and delivered directly to the importer.

However, even when the distance is short, distribution still requires coordination.

Understanding how this stage works helps importers avoid delays, manage costs, and ensure goods are delivered efficiently.

## From release to delivery

Once a shipment is released, it becomes available for collection.

At this stage, arrangements must be made to:

- Collect the cargo from the port or terminal  
- Transport it to the final destination  
- Coordinate timing with receiving parties  

While this may seem straightforward, timing is important.

Delays in collection can lead to additional charges or congestion at the collection point.

## Direct delivery vs staged delivery

In many local shipments, goods move directly from the port to the customer.

This is common where:

- The importer has space to receive the goods  
- The shipment is manageable in size  
- Delivery can be completed immediately  

However, not all shipments can move this way.

Staged delivery may be required where:

- Goods need to be delivered in parts  
- The receiving location cannot accommodate the full shipment at once  
- Coordination is required across multiple delivery points  

In these cases, distribution becomes more structured and may involve temporary storage or scheduling over time.

## Coordination and timing

Distribution is not only about transport—it is about coordination.

This includes:

- Aligning collection times with port or terminal operations  
- Ensuring trucks and drivers are available  
- Confirming that delivery locations are ready to receive goods  

If any part of this coordination breaks down, delays can occur.

Even short delays can affect costs, especially where storage or handling charges begin to apply.

## Handling considerations

Different types of cargo may require different handling approaches.

This may include:

- Palletized vs loose cargo  
- Fragile or sensitive goods  
- Heavy or oversized items  

Proper handling ensures that goods arrive in the same condition in which they were cleared.

## The role of logistics providers

A logistics provider can manage the movement of goods after clearance by coordinating:

- Collection from NCP or GFT  
- Transportation to the delivery point  
- Scheduling and routing of deliveries  
- Communication between all parties involved  

This reduces the burden on the importer and helps ensure that goods move efficiently from release to final destination.

## In simple terms

Distribution is the step that moves goods from clearance to use.

Even in a relatively small geographic area, it requires planning, timing, and coordination.

When managed properly, it ensures that shipments move quickly, safely, and without unnecessary cost.
